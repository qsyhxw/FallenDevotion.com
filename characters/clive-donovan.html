Clive Donovan is the central figure in Fallen Devotion - your childhood best friend who died three years ago and has mysteriously returned. His character explores themes of eternal return, obsessive devotion, and the tension between Apollonian order and Dionysian chaos from Nietzsche's philosophy.

The developer describes him as being "reborn, split between two natures, a personification of the eternal return and the balance - or clash - between the Apollonian and Dionysian spirits." Understanding Clive means understanding both the philosophical framework that inspired his creation and the emotional core that makes him resonate with players.

Quick Facts:

  • Full Name: Clive Donovan
  • Role: Main character, MC's childhood best friend
  • Status: Deceased (three years prior), mysteriously returned
  • Character Type: Yandere / Dark Romance
  • Philosophical Symbol: Eternal Return, Dionysian Spirit
  • Voice Acting: Partially voiced (in development)

Backstory & The Three-Year Mystery

Childhood Friendship

Clive and the player character (MC) were childhood best friends, sharing a deep bond that would later become the foundation for the game's exploration of love, loss, and obsession. The exact details of their early relationship are revealed gradually through the narrative, with players experiencing fragments of memories that showcase the warmth and purity of their connection.

The Death & Misunderstandings

Three years before the game's events, Clive died under circumstances that remain deliberately ambiguous in the demo. What is clear is that misunderstandings between Clive and the MC preceded his death, creating a complex web of guilt and unresolved emotions. The MC's guilt became so overwhelming that they couldn't bring themselves to attend Clive's funeral - a decision that haunts them throughout the story.

Note: The specific cause and circumstances of Clive's death are not explicitly detailed in the current demo version. The game focuses more on the emotional aftermath and philosophical implications than on forensic details.

The Return

The game begins with signs of Clive's presence becoming impossible to ignore. It starts with vivid nightmares that leave the MC breathless, then escalates to strange occurrences - the feeling of being watched, the weight of an inexplicable presence. According to the developer, the MC canonically experiences flashbacks of "what was left of his body beneath that cold, sterile, white cloth," showing the traumatic nature of these memories.

When Clive does return, something fundamental has changed. The official description states: "His love for you, once warm and pure, now feels as sharp as a blade." This transformation is central to understanding his character and the game's exploration of how death and resurrection might alter a person's nature.

Personality & Psychological Profile

The "Reborn" Nature

Clive is described by the developer as being "reborn, split between two natures." This duality is not just a narrative device but a fundamental aspect of how players experience his character. He embodies contradictions - tenderness and danger, devotion and obsession, the desire to protect and the impulse to possess.

Yandere Characteristics

Clive fits the yandere archetype - a character whose love is so intense it becomes dangerous and obsessive. Player feedback consistently describes him using terms like:

  • Devoted: His commitment to the MC is absolute and unwavering
  • Longing: A deep yearning that transcends even death
  • Worshiping: He places the MC on a pedestal, viewing them as his savior
  • Possessive: His love comes with an intense need to keep the MC close
  • Obsessive: His thoughts and actions revolve entirely around the MC

A particularly revealing developer comment describes Clive's mindset: "Feed into his delusions, and he'll tell you it's meant to be - you were meant to be together from the very beginning. In the end, you're the light he's been missing all along, the only thing keeping him sane. Maybe, just maybe, the gods felt so sorry for his fate that they sent you as his savior."

Gentleness vs. Intensity

Despite his obsessive tendencies, Clive is capable of remarkable gentleness. The developer describes how he would comfort the MC after nightmares: "He'd wake you up in the most gentle way imaginable - holding you close, caressing your face, guiding you through grounding exercises." He might whisper reassurances that "as long as you're with him, it won't ever happen again."

This duality creates what players describe as a "sweet, darling, sad boy" who simultaneously evokes protective instincts and wariness. Many players report feeling "awful for Clive and what happened to him," wanting to protect him even while recognizing the potentially dangerous nature of his devotion.

Developer Warning:

The developer includes a crucial note in character discussions: "Smack him back into balance, do NOT let the Dionysian take over." This suggests that player choices can influence whether Clive's Dionysian (chaotic, passionate) nature overwhelms his Apollonian (ordered, rational) side - with potentially serious consequences.

Emotional Complexity

Players consistently praise Clive's emotional depth. Comments like "I can see he's pretty complex" and "It's been a few days and I'm still thinking of Clive" reflect how the character stays with players beyond the gameplay experience. His capacity to evoke genuine sadness, protectiveness, and conflicted feelings demonstrates sophisticated character writing.

The game explores his inner world through what appears to be his perspective in certain scenes, with one player noting the clever use of "the film theme to convey how you're flicking through his memories." This narrative technique allows players to understand Clive's psychology from the inside, creating empathy even when his actions might be concerning.

Philosophical Themes & Symbolism

The developer explicitly states that "Nietzsche's philosophy is what inspired me into creating this game, particularly ideologies from Thus Spoke Zarathustra and The Birth of Tragedy." Clive serves as the primary vehicle for exploring these philosophical concepts within the narrative.

Eternal Return (Ewige Wiederkunft)

Clive is described as "a personification of the eternal return" - one of Nietzsche's most challenging concepts. Eternal return poses the question: What if you had to live your life over and over again, infinitely, in exactly the same way? Would you embrace this or despair?

In-Game Application:

Clive's return from death can be understood as a literal manifestation of eternal return. He has been given - or perhaps cursed with - a second chance, forced to confront the same relationship, the same love, the same pain. The question becomes: Will things play out differently, or are they doomed to repeat? Can love survive being lived twice?

Apollonian vs. Dionysian

In "The Birth of Tragedy," Nietzsche describes two opposing forces in art and human nature:

Apollonian (Apollo)

  • • Order and rationality
  • • Self-control and restraint
  • • Individual identity
  • • Clarity and boundaries
  • • Dream-like beauty

Dionysian (Dionysus)

  • • Chaos and passion
  • • Loss of inhibition
  • • Dissolution of self
  • • Breaking boundaries
  • • Ecstatic intoxication

Clive embodies "the balance - or clash - between the Apollonian and Dionysian spirits." His character is pulled between:

  • Apollonian tendencies: His gentle care for the MC, his desire to comfort and protect, his moments of clarity and self-awareness
  • Dionysian tendencies: His obsessive passion, his inability to accept boundaries, his willingness to transcend even death for love, his potential for violence

The developer's warning not to "let the Dionysian take over" suggests that the game allows players to influence which force dominates Clive's character - potentially leading to different endings based on whether players encourage his restraint or his abandon.

Love Beyond Death

The philosophical framework transforms what could be a simple ghost story into an exploration of whether love can survive - or be transformed by - death itself. Clive's return forces both him and the player to confront questions about the nature of devotion: Is love that persists beyond death beautiful or terrifying? When does devotion become delusion? Can you truly love someone if you can't let them go?

Relationship Dynamics

With the MC (Player Character)

Clive's relationship with the MC forms the emotional core of Fallen Devotion. It's characterized by:

  • Unconditional Devotion: Clive views the MC as "the light he's been missing all along, the only thing keeping him sane"
  • Second Chances: The game explores "the lengths someone would go for a second chance" at love
  • Possessiveness: He functions like a "guard dog," protective but intensely possessive
  • Comfort and Care: Despite his intensity, he shows genuine tenderness in how he cares for the MC
  • Manipulation Through Love: The developer hints that Clive might induce certain situations "to force the two of you to finally talk"

Relationship Dynamic Note: The developer clarifies that Clive would love the MC "no matter what," including being supportive of trans identities or other personal characteristics. His devotion is portrayed as unwavering, though the healthiness of such absolute devotion is part of what the game questions.

Tips for Interacting with Clive

Understanding His Nature

Success in Clive's routes comes from understanding the balance between encouraging his devotion and preventing his Dionysian nature from overwhelming his better judgment. The developer's advice to "not let the Dionysian take over" is key.

Core Principle:

Your choices should aim to "smack him back into balance" when his obsessive tendencies become too intense, while still showing care and acknowledgment of his feelings. Complete rejection may lead to bad endings, but complete acceptance of unhealthy behavior likely will too.

Choice Philosophy

Based on the philosophical framework and player experiences:

  • Honesty vs. Evasion: Clive responds to genuine emotion, so honest dialogue choices may yield better results than trying to manipulate or avoid difficult conversations
  • Setting Boundaries: The game seems to reward establishing healthy boundaries rather than either complete submission or total rejection
  • Acknowledging the Past: Choices that address the misunderstandings and guilt from before his death appear important
  • Balancing Forces: Look for options that encourage both his caring side (Apollonian) and acknowledge his passion (Dionysian) without letting either extreme dominate

Endings Approach

Players have confirmed multiple endings in the demo. While specific choices aren't detailed here to avoid excessive spoilers, consider:

  • Playing through at least twice with different philosophical approaches
  • Saving before major decision points to explore alternatives
  • Paying attention to how Clive reacts to your choices - his responses provide clues
  • Considering what each choice means in terms of the eternal return concept

Emotional Preparation

Many players report strong emotional reactions to Clive's story. His route deals with themes of grief, guilt, and loss that can be genuinely affecting. Take breaks if needed, and remember that the intensity of emotion is part of what makes the experience meaningful. As one player noted: "I love when stories make my heart soar and although I was emotional throughout, I was so happy while playing this."
